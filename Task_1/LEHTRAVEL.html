Welcome to Leh

Located at a height of 11,500 feet, Leh finds place in the bucket list of many travel enthusiasts. It will leave you in awe with its iconic monasteries and picturesque landscapes. Experience the beauty of Leh, a high-desert city in the Himalayas, known for its Buddhist sites and stunning landscapes.

The snow-clad peaks and the crystal-clear lakes will leave you spellbound. From adventurous activities, wildlife exploration, and museums, to peaceful monasteries, palaces, and valleys, there are plenty of activities to enjoy.

Famous for the delicious traditional and non-traditional cuisines served here. Some of the must-try delicacies in the city are Tigmo, Khambir, Thupka, Mokthuk, Chang, Paba and Tangur among others.

Top Attractions

Leh Palace

Also known as 'Lhachen Palkhar', this erstwhile royal palace is famous for its magnificent Tibetan architecture, and offers an insight into the local Buddhist culture.

Shanti Stupa

Considered as the epitome of peace, this white-domed magnificent Stupa was built in 1991 as a part of the peace pagoda mission and houses ancient relics of the Buddhist culture.

Hemis Monastery

The largest and wealthiest monastery in Ladakh, known for its annual festival and rich cultural heritage.

Magnetic Hill

Perched at a height of 14,000 feet, this mysterious hill is famous for its gravity-defying phenomenon that causes cars to roll uphill.

Best Time To Visit Leh

Perfect summer destination

MAY - JUNE

Peak season

  • What To Expect: Clear blue skies with cold temperatures, around 5C to 7C.

  • Things You'll Love: Sightseeing and the surreal vistas of snow-capped mountains.

    • JUL - AUG

    Off-season

  • What To Expect: Landslides and cloud bursts due to heavy rainfall.

  • Things You'll Love: Rain-washed mountain sceneries and heavy off-season discounts.

    • SEPT - FEB

    Moderate-season

  • What To Expect: Extremely chilly weather with freezing nights.

  • Things You'll Love: Walking on the frozen lakes.
